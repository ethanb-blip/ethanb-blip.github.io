2. Nitrogen Cycle & Water Quality

The Nitrogen Cycle

Fish waste produces ammonia. Beneficial bacteria convert ammonia → nitrite → nitrate.

  • Ammonia (toxic)
  • Nitrite (toxic)
  • Nitrate (less toxic, removed with water changes)

Cycling the Tank

Before adding fish, the aquarium must grow beneficial bacteria to process waste.

Testing Water

  • pH
  • Ammonia
  • Nitrite
  • Nitrate
  • Hardness
  • Temperature

Troubleshooting

  • Ammonia spike → reduce feeding
  • High nitrate → water change
  • Low pH → buffering or substrate changes

3. Filtration

Types of Filtration

  • Mechanical – removes debris
  • Biological – bacteria break down waste
  • Chemical – removes toxins using media

Filter Types

  • Canister filter
  • Hang-on-back (HOB)
  • Sponge filter
  • Internal filter

Pros & Cons

Canister filters are powerful but expensive. Sponge filters are cheap and great for shrimp or fry.

Maintenance

Rinse filter media in tank water, not tap water.

4. Planning Your Aquarium

Tank Size

Larger tanks are usually more stable and easier for beginners.

Substrate

  • Gravel – common and easy
  • Sand – good for bottom fish
  • Soil – best for planted tanks

Decorations

  • Driftwood
  • Rocks
  • Caves
  • Plants

Lighting

Lighting affects plant growth and algae levels.
